The Ultimate Guide to CNC Turned Parts

Materials, Processes & Applications in Modern Manufacturing

← Back to Blog

CNC turned parts are cylindrical components created through computer numerical control (CNC) turning processes. These precision components are essential in numerous industries, from automotive to medical devices, due to their exceptional accuracy and surface finishes. In this comprehensive guide, we'll explore everything you need to know about CNC turned parts.

What Are CNC Turned Parts?

CNC turned parts are components manufactured using CNC lathes, where a workpiece rotates while cutting tools shape the material. This process creates parts with rotational symmetry, including shafts, bushings, pins, and other cylindrical components. Unlike CNC milling where the tool moves along multiple axes, turning involves rotating the workpiece against stationary cutting tools.

Materials Used for CNC Turned Parts

The selection of materials for cnc turned parts depends on the specific application requirements, including strength, corrosion resistance, thermal properties, and cost considerations. Here are the most commonly used materials:

Metals

  • Aluminum Alloys (6061, 7075): Lightweight with excellent machinability and corrosion resistance. Ideal for automotive and aerospace applications.
  • Stainless Steel (304, 316): Superior corrosion resistance and strength. Commonly used in medical devices and food processing equipment.
  • Carbon Steel: High strength and wear resistance. Suitable for industrial machinery components.
  • Titanium: Exceptional strength-to-weight ratio and biocompatibility. Extensively used in aerospace and medical implants.
  • Brass and Bronze: Excellent electrical conductivity and corrosion resistance. Frequently used in electrical connectors and marine applications.

Plastics

  • PEEK: High-temperature resistance and chemical inertness. Used in demanding aerospace and medical applications.
  • PTFE (Teflon): Low friction and chemical resistance. Ideal for seals and bearings.
  • Delrin: High stiffness and low friction. Commonly used in precision gears and components.

CNC Turning Processes

Different CNC turning processes are employed based on part complexity and production requirements:

Horizontal CNC Turning

The most common turning method where the workpiece is mounted horizontally between centers or in a chuck. This process is ideal for shafts, bushings, and other cylindrical parts.

Vertical CNC Turning

Used for large, heavy parts where horizontal setup would be impractical. The workpiece is mounted vertically, and the cutting tools approach from the side.

Swiss CNC Turning

Specialized technique for long, slender parts with complex geometries. The workpiece is guided through a bushing, providing exceptional support and enabling high-precision machining of small diameter parts.

Multi-spindle CNC Turning

Efficient production method where multiple spindles work simultaneously to create several identical parts. Ideal for high-volume production runs of simple turned components.

Quality Considerations for CNC Turned Parts

When sourcing cnc turned parts, several quality factors should be evaluated:

  1. Dimensional Accuracy: Ensure the supplier can meet your tolerance requirements consistently.
  2. Surface Finish: Different applications require specific surface roughness values.
  3. Material Certification: Verify material composition and properties with proper certifications.
  4. Process Control: Look for suppliers with robust quality management systems.
  5. Inspection Capabilities: Comprehensive measurement and testing capabilities are essential.
